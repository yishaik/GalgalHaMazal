Overview

The system is divided into four main sub-systems: (1) Control Box – Power and Safety, (2) Control Box – Voltage Distribution, (3) Electronic Control Board, (4) Wheel Components – LED ring, matrix, and sensors. The design emphasizes safety, maintenance flexibility, and electrical quality (multiple power injections, a thick common GND, and clear color coding).

Control Box – Power and Safety

  • IEC C14 inlet, main fuses, and emergency switch.
  • Two separate power supplies: 24V (WS2814 ring) and 5V (matrix/controllers).
  • Separation of voltage rails, common GND for the entire system.

Control Box – Voltage Distribution

  • Bus Bars for 24V, 5V, and GND.
  • Fuses at the power supply output before the bus bar.
  • Durable field connectors (IP65) for distribution to thick cables.

Electronic Control Board

  • Arduino Uno for the LED ring, ESP32 for sensors, and ESP32 for the matrix.
  • Pull-up/series resistors, filtering capacitors, and careful GND layout.
  • Short and shielded data connection (WS281x DATA) to reduce noise coupling.

Wheel Components

  • WS2814 24V ring with 5 power injection points along its ~10m circumference.
  • 32×32 LED matrix (WS2812B 5V) in the center.
  • Hall sensors for speed/direction detection and a light triangle for stopping.

Quick Wiring Guidelines

  • Keep DATA lines as short as possible (≤1m), mark with green color.
  • Ensure power injections every ~2m for the ring; V+ red, GND black.
  • Maintain a thick common GND (2.5mm²) for all voltage rails.
  • Add a 330Ω series resistor to the DATA line and a 1000µF capacitor across 5V on the matrix.
